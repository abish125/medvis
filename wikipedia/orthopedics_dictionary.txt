noun

orthopedics in Medicine Expand


orthopedics or·tho·pe·dics or or·tho·pae·dics (ôr'thə-pē'dĭks)n.  The branch of medicine that deals with the prevention and correction of injuries or disorders of the skeletal system and associated muscles, joints, and ligaments. or'tho·pe'dic adj.

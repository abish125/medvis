Pyelonephritis /paɪəloʊnəˈfraɪtɪs/ (from Greek πύελο|ς pýelo|s, "basin" + νεφρ|ός nepʰrós, "kidney" + suffix -itis suggesting "inflammation") is an inflammation of the kidney parenchyma, calyces, and pelvis.[1] It is commonly caused by bacterial infection that has spread up the urinary tract or travelled through the bloodstream to the kidneys.[2]

Codeine or 3-methylmorphine (a naturally occurring methylated morphine) is an opiate used for its stimulant, analgesic, antitussive, antidiarrheal, antihypertensive, anxiolytic,[citation needed] antidepressant,[citation needed] sedative and hypnotic properties. It is also used to suppress premature labor contractions, myocardial infarction, and has many other potential and indicated uses. It is often sold as a salt in the form of either codeine sulfate or codeine phosphate in the United States, Australia and Canada; codeine hydrochloride is more common worldwide and the citrate, hydroiodide, hydrobromide, tartrate, and other salts are also seen.[2]
